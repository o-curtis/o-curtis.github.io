Olivia Curtis

I am a graduate student at the Boston University Department of Astronomy and Institute for Astrophysical Research. I received my B.S. in physics and B.A. in Mathematics from the University of South Florida in 2018.

I am using General Adversarial Networks (GANs), a relatively new unsupervised machine learning algorithm, to generate a mock void catalog of unprecedented size. I will use this catalog to investigate cosmic voids and use them as a laboratory for tests of dark energy.

I also work with Professor Tereasa Brainerd studying the large-scale structure in the Illustris-TNG Simulation. Currently, we are working on a project analyzing how galaxies found within cosmic voids differ morphologically from those found within galaxy clusters.
Illustris Simulation
May 2nd, 2021

Below is a video I recently made. The video shows the dark matter density of the Illustris TNG300-3 simulation at redshift 0. Each frame of the video displays a different z axis of the simulation box. The density map is 2048^3 pixels, and all of the 1024^3 particles were mapped to the mesh with a Cloud-in-cell routine. Huge thanks to my collegue Bryanne McDonough for pulling the particle data. Original HD video is available upon request!
